The 9 layers of hell Dante's Inferno:

Locations:

Limbo: The unbaptized and virtuous pagans, such as Homer, Socrates, and Aristotle, reside here. Their punishment is a hopeless longing for God.

Lust: Adulterers are blown around by uncontrollable, violent winds, symbolizing their inability to control their desires.

Gluttony: The overindulgent exist in a vile, eternal mess of icy, freezing slush and sludge.

Greed: The greedy (hoarders and wasters) are divided into two groups, struggling against each other by rolling massive, heavy boulders.

Anger: The angry and sullen fight each other on the surface of the river Styx, while the sullen choke beneath the muddy water.

Heresy: Those who rejected the church's teachings are eternally imprisoned in burning, fiery tombs.

Violence: Divided into three rings:

    Others: Violent murderers, tyrants, and robbers are immersed in a boiling river of blood.
    Self: Those who harmed themselves (suicides) are transformed into twisted, gnarled trees.
    God: Those who harmed God or nature are kept in a burning desert.

Fraud: This circle, known as the Malebolge ("Evil Pockets"), contains fraudsters: seducers, flatterers, simoniacs, sorcerers, corrupt politicians, hypocrites, thieves, evil counselors, sowers of discord, and falsifiers. Punishments vary by pocket, including being submerged in excrement, whipped by demons, walking in heavy cloaks, and being plagued with disease.

Treachery: Divided into four regions within a frozen lake called Cocytus:

    Caina: Traitors to family (frozen in ice up to necks).
    Antenora: Traitors to country/political party.
    Ptolomaea: Traitors to guests (frozen in ice with heads bent backward).
    Judecca: Traitors to masters/benefactors (fully submerged in ice).
